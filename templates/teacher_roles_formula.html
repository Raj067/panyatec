Formula 1

For form four subjects with practical paper formula 1 can be calculated as follow.
     
    p = ((p1 + p2) / 150) x 100%
     
for example: if student x has 75% in paper 1 and 40% in paper 2 then his final marks using this formula will be as follow,
     
    p = ((75 + 40) / 150) x 100%
     
    p = 76.7%
     
For form six subjects with practical paper particularly biology physics chemistry and subject with three papers formula 1 can be calculated as follow.
     
    p = ((p1 + p2 + p3) / 250) x 100%
     
for example: if student x has 75% in paper 1, 80% in paper 2 and 40% in paper 3 then his final marks using this formula will be as follow,
     
    p = ((75 + 80 + 40) / 250) x 100%
     
    p = 78.0%
     

Formula 2

For form four subjects with practical paper formula 2 can be calculated as follow.
     
    p = (p1 + p2 x 2) / 2
     
for example: if student x has 75% in paper 1 and 40% in paper 2 then his final marks using this formula will be as follow,
     
    p = (75 + 40 x 2) / 2
     
    p = 77.5%
     
For form six subjects with practical paper particularly biology physics chemistry and subject with three papers formula 2 can be calculated as follow.
     
    p = (p1 + p2 + p3 x 2) / 3
     
for example: if student x has 75% in paper 1, 80% in paper 2 and 40% in paper 3 then his final marks using this formula will be as follow,
     
    p = (75 + 80 + 40 x 2) / 3
     
    p = 78.3%
     

Formula 3

For form six subjects with two paper like Language and Geography without practical the formula used could be as follow.
     
    p = (p1 + p2 ) / 2
     
for example: if student x has 75% in paper 1 and 80% in paper 2 then his final marks using this formula will be as follow,
     
    p = (75 + 80) / 2
     
    p = 77.5%
